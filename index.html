Pro Tips

Get More Out of Every Search

Follow the Deal, Not the Destination

The best deal is often not directly from your origin to your end destination. Consider a positioning flight to a hub airport (Ex. BWI to JFK for a better business class deal to Paris), or fly nearby and take a budget carrier the rest of the way (Ex. fly to London, then RyanAir to Italy).

Be Flexible with Your Dates

The difference of one day can have a huge impact on points price, even more so than cash prices. Weekends cost more than weekdays, holidays and peak travel times will be pricier, and the best premium availability opens 10 to 12 months out.

Pay Attention to Transfer Bonuses

Credit card and airline partners occasionally offer transfer bonuses of 10 to 50%+ where you get extra points when moving them to an airline program. I usually don't recommend transferring unless you have a specific flight in mind and have confirmed seat availability first, especially when using an aggregator tool.

Honest take: Points aggregator tools are improving but still miss many programs, lag on availability, and carry annual costs that may not be worth it if you travel once or twice a year. An expert who knows the sweet spots will often find better deals faster.
